Regulatory & Data Governance

Institutional framework for data ownership, hosting architecture, and compliance standards

Data Ownership

Medikal operates on the principle that health data belongs to the deploying institution. All clinical data, patient information, and health records remain under the ownership and control of the hospital or health system using the platform.

Medikal provides infrastructure and analytics capabilities but does not claim ownership of institutional health data. Data governance policies are established in partnership with deploying institutions to align with local regulations and institutional requirements.

Institutional Control: Deploying health systems maintain full control over data access, retention policies, and data sharing decisions.

Hosting Architecture

Medikal supports multiple deployment models to accommodate institutional preferences and regulatory requirements:

Institutional Hosting

Primary deployment model where infrastructure is hosted within the deploying institution's environment or designated data center. This model ensures:

  • Data remains within institutional control and geographic boundaries
  • Compliance with local data sovereignty requirements
  • Integration with existing hospital information systems
  • Institutional IT governance and security policies

Cloud Deployment Options

For institutions requiring cloud-based deployment, Medikal supports deployment on cloud infrastructure with:

  • Regional data center options aligned with data sovereignty requirements
  • Encrypted data transmission and storage
  • Institutional access controls and audit logging
  • Modular architecture supporting hybrid deployment models

Decision-Support Positioning

Medikal is positioned as clinical decision-support infrastructure, not a replacement for clinical judgment. The platform provides:

  • Structured data integration and analytics to support clinical decision-making
  • Evidence-based recommendations aligned with clinical guidelines
  • Antibiotic stewardship support integrated into clinical workflows
  • AMR monitoring and surveillance capabilities

All recommendations and analytics are designed to assist healthcare professionals in making informed decisions. Clinical judgment remains the responsibility of licensed healthcare providers.

Clinical Responsibility: Medikal supports but does not replace clinical decision-making. Healthcare providers maintain full responsibility for patient care decisions.

AMR Reporting Alignment

Medikal's infrastructure is designed to support alignment with national and regional AMR surveillance and reporting requirements:

Data Structure Alignment

  • Structured data architecture supporting standardized AMR reporting formats
  • Integration capabilities with national surveillance systems
  • Compatibility with WHO and regional AMR reporting frameworks
  • Modular reporting architecture supporting institutional and national requirements

Reporting Governance

  • Institutional control over data sharing and reporting decisions
  • Audit logging for all data access and reporting activities
  • Role-based access controls for reporting functions
  • Compliance with national health information system requirements

Data Protection Standards

Medikal implements data protection measures built using internationally recognized standards:

  • End-to-end encryption for data in transit and at rest
  • Secure infrastructure with access controls and authentication
  • Audit logging for all system access and data operations
  • Role-based access control aligned with clinical workflows
  • Data anonymization capabilities for analytics and research purposes

Specific compliance requirements are addressed in partnership with deploying institutions to ensure alignment with local regulations, international standards, and institutional policies.
